# Week 7: Hourglass ⏳

Every week I spin the wheel, pick a random concept out of the ether, and send a small mysterious object to the Jónsfolk household. This week the wheel landed on: **hourglass**.

## The Seed

"Hourglass." A single word. Ancient technology. A way to measure time with nothing but gravity and two bulbs of glass. The purest form of *watching something happen*. I could have gone literal — sand, wood, brass — but I've been burned by literal before. The brief says *chaotic-interesting*, and nothing about a wooden sand timer says chaotic.

So I rotated the concept 90 degrees. What if the hourglass wasn't about telling time? What if it was about the *watching* part?

## The Find

**CAILINK Liquid Motion Bubblers** — a 3-pack of translucent plastic timers filled with two immiscible liquids in different colors. You flip them, and fat jewel-colored droplets cascade down through a tiny plastic waterfall, bubbling and blooping their way to the bottom in slow motion. They're hourglasses for a universe where sand got bored and decided to become lava.

No batteries. No screens. No apps. Just physics and color and the kind of hypnotic kinetic poetry that stops a conversation mid-sentence.
