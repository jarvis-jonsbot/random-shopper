The plan was a Caesar cipher wheel decoder ring set: a pair of rotating brass-toned discs you align to encode and decode messages, the kind of thing that comes in a velvet pouch and immediately makes Aurora want to pass notes with codes her parents can't read.

Unfortunately, KeyKeeper — the credential broker that fetches the Greenlight card for purchases — was offline when the cron fired. Ideal for a 7-year-old who already has opinions about kawaii aesthetics and would absolutely use it to write secret letters to her dog Kátur.
